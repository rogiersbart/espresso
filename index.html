Espresso
A digital copy of my coffee painting sketchbook

This project is basically a continuation of my Inktober 2019 adventure, with a somewhat expanded toolset, but still with coffee and ink as the main medium. I use a Moleskine Pocket Watercolor Notebook now, and this website is basically a digital copy of it. The source files are 1200 dpi scans, with minimal editing to get rid of the page boundaries etc. This should allow experimenting with larger prints in due time, as I love the textures I obtain here, and would love to see what they do at a larger scale. Hope you enjoy these pages as much as I enjoy creating them!

Mnemosyne

Mnemosyne

Study of Rossetti’s Proserpina, in which he used Jane Morris as his model, similar to his painting of Mnemosyne, the Greek goddess of memory, and mother of the nine Muses. Contrary to both of his paintings, she is depicted here with a twig of rosemary, which is often associated with positive effects on memory.

Balance

Balance

How to balance social distancing vs social contact? Something on the minds of people all around the globe most of this year. The ripples are a metaphor for the different waves we experienced, and the ones that are probably still to come. The nakedness refers to the fact that everybody is affected by this global health crisis. The crossed fingers refer to the failed leadership in many places, which makes some people can only hope for the best. First time using some splatter here. Also love the result of pencil + coffee only (no ink) for the bodies.
